With mobility patterns in hand, we overlayed the changes in major economic sectors experienced during the pandemic in order to discover the relationship between mobility trends and retail sales.

In doing so, we found that most retail sectors had a proportional response to mobility. In contrast, we found that food and beverage stores, grocery stores, liquor stores, health and personal care, pharmacy, electronic sales showed an independence of mobility. At the points when a significant decrease was seen in mobility, and most economic sectors, these sectors saw increases.
